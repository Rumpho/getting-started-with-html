What I learned about the WWW and HTML

In this introductory section I learned some of the basics of the World Wide Web, what HTML (HyperText Markup Language) is, and that it is the main type of document on the web. I now also better understand what tags are and, for the first time, learned about attributes. More details are provided below.

I understand that I access the internet through my computer browser (Chrome, in my case) and the internet in turn accesses information from servers; information written in HTML. We see the finished text on our screens, but the information also contains all the mark-ups, refs to other docs, e.g., images and video, and links to other pages.

Markup tags typically have an opening and closing tag, but there are also void tags, e.g., the line break, br, and image tag, img, which do not have or need a closing tag. There are inline tags such as b, em, a, span and img. There are also block tags such as p and div.

I was especially pleased to learn that extra character spaces and extra white lines are automatically removed in the final text! I am perplexed by what the attribute src really means with the image tag, but I also learned that programmers can't remember everything and I probably can just look this up.
